World Trade in the HO Model

It may be helpful to first recall Demand Diagonals, Production Possibilities Frontier and World Trade in the Ricardian Model

World Trade in the HO Model

Z = Imports, E = Exports, EVR = Vietnamese Rice Exports, EJM = Japanese Motorcycle Exports, ZVM = Vietnamese Motorcycle Imports, ZJR = Japanese Rice Imports

The HO Model

The Heckscher-Ohlin (HO) model operates effectively the same as the Ricardian model except the PPF is determined in a more realistic (and by extension complex) manner. The HO model has a curved PPF because (amongst other factors) it becomes incredibly difficult to use every resource for rice production instead of motorcycle production (Ex. how do you use a welding torch for rice cultivation instead of making a motorcycle?).

For this reason, point B is no longer a country putting ALL of it's resources towards the production of the product it has comparative advantage in. Instead it finds the most efficient level of production based on the world price of the product. B is plotted where the world price line (the dashed one) intersects the PPF.

The Discrepancy in Imports

But why does Vietnam import a minority of its motorcycles when Japan is still importing most of it's rice? If you look at the curve of the PPFs you might notice that Japan's is sharper than Vietnam's. This represents that Japan has a higher opportunity cost for producing rice instead of motorcycles. They produce vastly more motorcycles than rice for the same amount of resources. Vietnam on the other hand has a more balanced opportunity cost. Even when producing at point B (most efficient based on the world price), they still have enough resources to fulfill most of their motorcycle demand. The remainder (ZVM) is then imported. When Japan produces at it's point B it has practically no resources left for rice rice production, meaning it then has to import the majority (ZJR)
