*It is best to first recall the Production Possibilities Frontier

DD on the Ricardian Model

In the Ricardian model the PPF is a straight line because it assumes that technology is the only factor influencing the opportunity cost. DD is the Demand Diagonal, it represents the ratio an economy wishes to consume a product for a given quantity of the other product. Using the above example the DD is in effect answering the question "If a consumer in this economy is purchasing 5 units of rice, how many motorcycles are they purchasing?"

The takeaway (I guess?)

Where the Demand Diagonal intercepts the price line (in autarky this is the PPF) is the quantity that the economy will consume of each product under free trade.
