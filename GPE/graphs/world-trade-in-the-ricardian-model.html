World Trade in the Ricardian Model

It may be helpful to first recall Demand Diagonals and Production Possibilities Frontier

World Trade in the Riccardian Model

The lines

  • DD: the Demand Diagonal
  • Unlabeled solid: the Production Possibilities Frontier in autarky
  • Dashed line: the Production Possibilities Frontier with world trade

The points

  • Point A represents consumption in autarky
    • This point is on the Demand Diagonal because that is where the economy is in line with consumer demand
  • Point B represents complete specialization in a single product
    • In these cases that is motorcycles for Japan and rice for Vietnam because that is what they each respectively have a comparative advantage in.
  • Point C represents consumption when each economy trades its excess products to the other
    • This enables them to both consume beyond their domestic PPF.
    • This point is on the Demand Diagonal because that is where the economy is in line with consumer demand

Adding Imports and Exports Labels

Measuring Imports and Exports

Z = Imports, E = Exports, CVR = Vietnamese Rice Consumption, CJM = Japanese Motorcycle Consumption, ZVM = Vietnamese Motorcycle Imports, ZJR = Japanese Rice Imports (his graph labels kinda suck ass here so bear with me)

In the above graph we assume that each country is producing at point B (see previous section). Vietnam will consume rice until point C (CVR), Japan will consume motorcycles until point C (CJM). All additional product, not consumed domestically, is then exported (represented by EJM and EVR respectively).

Since each country is putting all if its resources into its most efficiently produced product (producing at B), they import all of their consumption of the other product. For Vietnam this means their consumption of motorcycles is entirely imported (ZVM). For Japan this means their consumption of rice is entirely imported (ZJR).
