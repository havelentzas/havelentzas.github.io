# Production Possibilities Frontier
The production possibilities frontier is a graph representing all possible quantities of products that may be produced when all factors of production are used with perfect efficiency.
## Graphically
The X and Y axis each represent the quantity produced of a given commodity. The graph displays the production capacity of one commodity for any given production level of the other.

Any point **beneath the curve** is inefficient. Any point **beyond the curve** is impossible.
# Slope
The slope of a PPF at any given point is called the [[Marginal Rate of Transformation]]. In other words it is the marginal "[[Opportunity Cost]]" of a commodity. It measures how much of good Y is given up for one more unit of good X or X for Y.
# Transformations
- Outward shift = growth in input availability or technological progress
- Inward shift = contraction in input availability
