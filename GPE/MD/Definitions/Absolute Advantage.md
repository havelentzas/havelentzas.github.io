# Absolute Advantage
One country can produce a product more efficiently than another (i.e. more cheaply). Therefore, that country will export to another country (assuming it is allowed to).

> [!example] Rice in Vietnam and Japan
> Vietnam may export rice at a price cheaper than Japan can produce domestically and hence export rice to Japan. Some may go hungry in Vietnam. Some economists justify this by saying the inflow of capital, rising wages, investment in industry and infrastructure, etc., will initiate a **reinforcing cycle of development**. In this way Vietnam entered the global economy. 
## Graphically
When the supply curve for country A is to the right of Country B's supply curve it means that at every price Country A supplies more of that commodity at every price.
