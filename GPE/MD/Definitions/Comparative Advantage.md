[[Absolute Advantage]] works in a one product model. **Comparative Advantage** assumes a two product model. This is the logical next step because countries would not want to accept imports if it could not reciprocate and export things to other countries as well. This is because countries desire a healthy [[Balance of Payments]] (i.e. balanced inflows and outflows of capital).

As graphed on the [[Production Possibilities Frontier]] there are tradeoffs that countries make in order to produce one unit of a commodity in terms of another commodity. For example to make one motorcycle you forgo making 3 units of rice, but another country only forgoes 2 units of rice per motorcycle. This trade off of X amount of one item produced for every Y amount of another is the [[Opportunity Cost]]. If you have the lower opportunity cost (in this case 2 rice per motorcycle) you have the comparative advantage.
