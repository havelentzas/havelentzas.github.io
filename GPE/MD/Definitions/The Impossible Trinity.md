The impossible trinity is a **theory** regarding exchange rates and monetary policy. It is called the impossible trinity because, while countries want all 3, the laws of economics make it so that only 2 out of 3 are possible at any one time.
# What economies want
Each side of the triangle represents what economies want. Everybody (in theory) wants: 
- Monetary independence
	- Gives them more control in managing their domestic economy
- Exchange rate stability
	- Creates certainty for business conducting cross-border deals. They can be certain they will be paid at the same value because the currency will not increase/decrease in value unexpectedly changing their return on investment. 
- Capital mobility
	- to enable foreign savings and investment
