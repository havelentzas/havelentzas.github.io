# Trade Diversion
Assume Brazil currently has tariffs on both Argentinian and El Salvadorian imports. El Salvador in this case has the lowest cost for this product. Trade diversion occurs when Brazil makes a PTA with Argentina, who has a higher cost. Before the PTA (i.e. with equal tariffs) it is expected that Brazil was trading with El Salvador because P<sup>S</sup> + T was the lowest price available. But with a PTA between Argentina and Brazil, Brazil will begin to trade with Argentina instead. This is the trade being diverted from El Salvador to Argentina because they are subject to different tariffs. 
# The Shapes
In this graph Brazil is **gaining** A, B, C, and D as [[Consumer Surplus]]. They are **loosing** A as [[Producer Surplus]]. They also loose both C and E which was the tariffs from El Salvador that they are no longer collecting because trade with El Salvador (theoretically) ends for this product. The net gain is B + D, but you will notice that those are quite small so E (the larger area) outweighs it. This results in a net loss from this trade diversion that does not happen with trade creation.
