# The lines
- **DD**: the [[Demand Diagonals|Demand Diagonal]]
- **Unlabeled solid**: the [[Production Possibilities Frontier]] (and by extension price line) in autarky
- **Dashed line**: the price line with world trade
# The points
- **Point A** represents consumption in autarky
	- This point is on the [[Demand Diagonals|Demand Diagonal]] because that is where the economy is in line with consumer demand
- **Point B** represents complete specialization in a single product
	- In these cases that is motorcycles for Japan and rice for Vietnam because that is what they each respectively have a comparative advantage in.
- **Point C** represents consumption when each economy trades its excess products to the other
	- This enables them to both consume beyond their domestic [[Production Possibilities Frontier|PPF]]. 
	- This point is on the [[Demand Diagonals|Demand Diagonal]] because that is where the economy is in line with consumer demand
# Adding Imports and Exports Labels

> [!img]+ Measuring Imports and Exports
> ![[Pasted image 20260323165145.png]]

*Z = Imports, E = Exports, CVR = Vietnamese Rice Consumption, CJM = Japanese Motorcycle Consumption, Z<sup>V</sup><sub>M</sub> = Vietnamese Motorcycle Imports, Z<sup>J</sup><sub>R</sub> = Japanese Rice Imports (his graph labels kinda suck ass here so bear with me)*

In the above graph we assume that each country is producing at point B (see previous section). Vietnam will consume **rice** until point C (CVR), Japan will consume **motorcycles** until point C (CJM). All additional product, not consumed domestically, is then exported (represented by EJM and EVR respectively). 

Since each country is putting all if its resources into its most efficiently produced product (producing at B), they import all of their consumption of the other product. For Vietnam this means their consumption of motorcycles is entirely imported (Z<sup>V</sup><sub>M</sub>). For Japan this means their consumption of rice is entirely imported (Z<sup>J</sup><sub>R</sub>).
