# The Areas/Shapes
## Without the tariff
When the price is at P<sup>W</sup>, shapes A, B, C, and D are all part of [[Consumer Surplus]]. 
## With the tariff
When the price is at P<sup>W</sup> + T:
- **A:** [[Producer Surplus]]
	- domestic producers regain the ability to sell to individuals who are willing to purchase rice at this higher price
- **B:** [[#Deadweight loss]]
- **C:** Revenue for the government by collecting the tariff
- **D:** [[#Deadweight loss]]
## Deadweight loss
*He emphasized this a lot in lectures*
When a tariff is imposed the price shifts from P<sup>W</sup> to P<sup>W</sup> + T. In the process, areas B and D are lost and not gained by anyone. Hence why they are called deadweight loss. Without the tariff this is part of [[Consumer Surplus]]. 
# Equation
The Net Welfare Effect (N), can be represented by the following equation.
$$
N = A + C – (A + B + C + D) = -(B + D)
$$
