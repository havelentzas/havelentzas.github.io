# Trade Creation
Assume Brazil currently has tariffs on both Argentinian and El Salvadorian imports. In this case Brazil would like to trade with Argentina because, even with tariffs, they have the lowest price. Reducing or removing tariffs on both countries equally would lead Brazil to still trade with Argentina, though **the volume (amount) traded will increase** due to the lower price. This is trade creation by removing tariffs with a preferential trade agreement. 
## The Shapes
The [[Consumer Surplus]] gained is shapes ABCD. It is NOT effected by El Salvadors price level with or without tariffs. This is because the lower bound of prices for this product is always determined by the lowest price, which El Salvador does not have in either case.

**Gains and loss for the Brazilian economy by signing this PTA
- **A**
	- Gain: [[Consumer Surplus]]
	- Loss: [[Producer Surplus]]
- **B**
	- Gain: [[Consumer Surplus]]
	- Loss: *none*
- **C**
	- Gain: [[Consumer Surplus]]
	- Loss: Government tariff revenue
- **D**
	- Gain: [[Consumer Surplus]]
	- Loss: *none*
Net welfare is a *gain* of B + D
